BMW M4 Description

Extended Information

Technical Specifications

    Engine Type: 3.0L Turbocharged Inline-6

    Power Output: 473 hp

    Torque: 406 lb-ft

    Transmission: 6-speed manual transmission

    Acceleration (0-60 mph): Approximately 4.1 seconds

    Top Speed: Electronically limited to 155 mph

    Fuel Economy (EPA Estimate): 15 mpg city / 22 mpg highway

    Weight: Approx. 3,800 lbs

Design

    Exterior: A wider stance with flared wheel arches, wider rear tires, and a more muscular appearance. A bold kidney grille, wider and more imposing compared to standard 4 Series models. Optional carbon fiber elements, including roof and mirror caps for reduced weight.

    Interior: A driver-focused cockpit with M-specific displays and materials. Sports seats wrapped in Merino leather, designed to offer superior support. High-quality materials throughout, with customizable options for trims and colors. Ambient lighting options and an M steering wheel with integrated performance controls.

Systems and Technology

    Infotainment System: BMW iDrive 7.0 or 8.0, depending on the model year, with a 12.3-inch digital display. Apple CarPlay and Android Auto integration. Available Gesture Control for touch-free control of media and navigation.

    Driver Assistance Features: Parking Assistant Plus: Features automatic parallel and perpendicular parking. Adaptive Cruise Control: Maintains a set speed and distance from the car ahead, enhancing long-distance driving.

    Performance Features: Launch Control: Ensures maximum acceleration during quick starts

Cons

    Price: The M4 is an expensive vehicle, especially the Competition version with the added options. The base price is already high, and adding extras like carbon fiber components and advanced tech packages increases the cost substantially.

    Ride Comfort: Due to the sport-tuned suspension, the M4 may have a stiffer ride compared to other luxury coupes, which could be uncomfortable on rough roads or for long commutes.

    Fuel Economy: With its high-performance engine, the M4's fuel economy is relatively low compared to more conventional cars in the same class. Frequent stops at the pump may be expected.
