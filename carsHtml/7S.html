BMW 7S Description

Extended Information

Technical Specifications

    Engine Type: 3.0L TwinPower Turbo Inline-6 with mild hybrid technology

    Power Output: 375 hp

    Torque: 398 lb-ft

    Transmission: All combustion models come with an 8-speed automatic transmission

    Acceleration (0-60 mph): 5.0 seconds

    Fuel Economy: ~25 mpg city / 31 mpg highway

    Cargo Space: 13.7 cubic feet for ICE models, slightly reduced for the i7 due to battery placement

Design

    Exterior: Front Fascia: Dominated by a large illuminated kidney grille and split-headlight design with optional crystal accents. Side Profile: Smooth and minimalist with flush door handles and elegant lines. Rear Design: Slim LED taillights emphasize the car’s width and luxury.

    Interior: Material Quality: Premium materials like Merino leather, open-pore wood, and crystal controls. Ambient Lighting: Customizable lighting strips integrated into the dashboard and doors. Theater Screen (Optional): A 31.3-inch 8K display folds down for rear passengers, turning the car into a mobile cinema.

Systems and Technology

    Infotainment System: iDrive 8.5 System: Features a curved 12.3-inch driver display and a 14.9-inch infotainment screen. Bowers & Wilkins Diamond Surround Sound System (optional).

    Features: Forward Collision Warning with Automatic Emergency Braking. Adaptive Air Suspension: Automatically adjusts ride height and comfort. Augmented Reality Navigation: Projects navigation directions directly onto the windshield. Integral Active Steering: Rear-wheel steering for improved maneuverability.

Cons

    Price: The 7-Series starts at a high price, and adding optional features can significantly increase the cost.

    Design Polarization: The bold exterior design, especially the split headlights and large grille, may not appeal to all buyers.

    Weight: Due to advanced systems and, in the case of the i7, a heavy battery, the car’s weight can impact agility compared to smaller sedans.

    Learning Curve for Technology: The abundance of tech features and the iDrive system may be overwhelming for less tech-savvy users.
